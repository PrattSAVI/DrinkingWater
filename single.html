Arsenic

SOURCE

Arsenic enters the water system through erosion of natural deposits; runoff from orchards; glass and electronics production wastes.

DESCRIPTION

An inorganic contaminant regulated under the Phase II/V Rules. Arsenic is a semi-metal element in the periodic table. It is odorless and tasteless. It can enter drinking water supplies from natural deposits in the earth or from agricultural and industrial practices. Some people who drink water containing arsenic in excess of the MCL over many years could experience skin damage or problems with their circulatory system, and may have an increased risk of getting cancer. Arsenic has been linked to a number of cancers. These include cancer of the bladder, lungs, skin, kidney, nasal passages, liver, and prostate.

WHAT ARE WE DOING ABOUT IT?
